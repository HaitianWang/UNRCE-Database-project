What is UNRCE?

The United Nations Regional Centre of Expertise (UNRCE) is an international initiative spearheaded by the United Nations. It aims to foster collaborative partnerships at regional levels to address the pressing challenges of sustainable development. Through education and research, RCEs promote and advance local sustainable development strategies tailored to the specific needs and challenges of their respective regions.

Our History

The journey of UNRCE began with the global call from the United Nations to address sustainability issues in an integrative manner. Recognizing the importance of collective efforts, the UN established the Regional Centres of Expertise as platforms to collaborate, learn, and adapt. Over the years, these centres have grown in number, strength, and impact, contributing significantly to regional sustainable development narratives and practices.

What is UNRCE WA?

The UNRCE for Western Australia (UNRCE WA) functions as the secretariat for the region, with UWA holding a pivotal role in its operations. UNRCE WA consists of two main hubs: Perth and the Great Southern, the latter being based at the UWA Albany Campus. As part of this dynamic network, UNRCE WA dedicates its efforts to foster partnerships, drive research, and promote educational initiatives that resonate with the unique sustainability challenges and opportunities found in Western Australia.
